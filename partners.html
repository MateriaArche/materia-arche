For research partners

We work best with teams that already have strong scientific capability and want a more disciplined upstream decision layer for candidate ranking, benchmark design, and validation planning.

What a good first project looks like

A good first project has a bounded scope, a defined candidate set, a meaningful decision deadline, and a realistic path to validation.

How we collaborate

We prefer clearly scoped work, explicit technical review points, and early alignment on confidentiality, data handling, and publication expectations.

What we need from partners

Useful starting inputs usually include the material class, the candidate universe, decision criteria, any existing baseline methods, and the practical constraints on validation.

The aim is not to replace deep lab expertise. It is to help excellent teams test fewer wrong things.
