What we do

We help advanced-materials teams reduce wasted experimental cycles by narrowing candidate sets before scarce lab time, instrument time, and engineering effort are committed.

Benchmark Audit

Stress-test an existing ranking or screening workflow. We identify where signal is real, where assumptions are weak, and what must be measured next.

Candidate Prioritisation Sprint

Rank a constrained candidate set against agreed technical criteria, with uncertainty-aware reasoning and a recommended validation matrix.

Managed Evidence Package

Coordinate modelling logic, decision criteria, and external validation planning for one focused materials question.

How we work

Our operating principle is simple: do not claim lift without a baseline. Materia Arche is built to improve experimental decisions, not to maximise model complexity. That means we use the least elaborate stack that can answer the question well, and we treat quantum methods as bounded tools rather than branding.

Classical baselines first

Every project begins with the strongest practical classical baseline available.

Quantum only where justified

We use quantum workflows where they are technically appropriate and where their contribution can be measured against a classical alternative.

Frozen benchmarks

We define the benchmark, inputs, and success criteria before evaluating performance.

Auditable outputs

Every ranking should produce a decision trail that a partner can inspect, challenge, and use.

Current focus

Materia Arche is currently focused on thin-film photovoltaics and adjacent advanced-materials problems where candidate ranking, stability-oriented test design, and evidence discipline matter more than broad platform claims.

Thin-film PV and perovskites

We are especially interested in materials-selection and validation questions where teams need a disciplined way to compare candidate systems, structure test matrices, and decide what is worth physical follow-up.

Adjacent materials opportunities

We also work on nearby materials-selection problems where the core challenge is the same: limited testing bandwidth, noisy evidence, and the need to make better decisions with less wasted effort.

Why teams work with us

Partner-ready outputs
