Method

Why this approach works

The goal is not to produce the most elaborate model. The goal is to improve the next experimental decision.

Classical baselines first

Every workflow starts from the strongest practical classical comparison available.

Ablation is mandatory

Where multiple methods are used, we test what each one actually adds.

Stability matters

Ranking is only useful if it connects to durability, process relevance, and validation design.

Outputs must be inspectable

A useful result is not just a score. It is a rationale that a partner can challenge and use.

Research partners

Built for research institutes and industrial R&D teams

Materia Arche is designed to complement strong experimental teams, not replace them. We are most useful upstream of scarce device fabrication and characterisation, where the cost of testing the wrong thing is high.

Good first projects are narrow

A bounded candidate set beats an open-ended innovation brief.

Interfaces should be clear

Decision criteria, available data, and validation constraints should be explicit.

Collaboration should be serious

Confidentiality, technical review, and publication expectations should be discussed early.

Open to collaboration with research institutes, advanced-materials teams, and industrial R&D groups working on defined candidate-selection problems.

Adjacent materials

Selective, not broad.

Our public focus is perovskite and thin-film PV. We will selectively support adjacent advanced-materials problems only where the core challenge is materially the same: stability-critical thin films and interfaces under limited testing bandwidth.

Future adjacent areas may include battery interfaces, membranes, and catalysis after the perovskite validation loop is established.

Start with a benchmarkable question.

The strongest first project is usually not the largest one. It is a constrained candidate set, a real decision deadline, and a validation path that matters.
